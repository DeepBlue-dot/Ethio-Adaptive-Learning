Reading a linear graph should connect back to the algebraic form:

1. Locate the `y`-intercept on the vertical axis.
2. Use two visible points to compute slope: `m = \\frac{\\Delta y}{\\Delta x}`.
3. Check whether the graphical behavior matches the sign and size of the slope.

Students should treat tables, equations, and graphs as interchangeable views of the same relationship rather than separate problem types.
