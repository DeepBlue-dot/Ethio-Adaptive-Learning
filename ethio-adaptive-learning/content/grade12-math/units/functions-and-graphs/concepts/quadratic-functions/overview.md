Quadratic functions describe relationships where the rate of change itself changes. Their graphs are parabolas, and students need to move comfortably between equation form, graph features, and factor-based reasoning.

Key forms to recognize:

- Standard form: `f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c`
- Vertex form: `f(x) = a(x - h)^2 + k`
- Factor form: `f(x) = a(x - r_1)(x - r_2)`

This concept depends on fluency with simpler function notation and graph interpretation from linear functions.
