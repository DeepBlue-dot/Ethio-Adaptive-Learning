In standard form, `f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c`, the coefficient `a` controls whether the parabola opens upward or downward and how narrow or wide it appears.

In vertex form, `f(x) = a(x - h)^2 + k`, the vertex is read directly as `(h, k)`.

Students should know when each form is most useful:

- standard form for general recognition,
- vertex form for transformations and turning points,
- factor form for roots and `x`-intercepts.
