Factoring connects algebraic manipulation to graph behavior.

If `f(x) = (x - r_1)(x - r_2)`, then the roots are `r_1` and `r_2` because those values make the function equal to zero.

When students factor `x^2 - 5x + 6` into `(x - 2)(x - 3)`, they should also recognize:

- the roots are `2` and `3`,
- the graph crosses the `x`-axis at `x = 2` and `x = 3`.
